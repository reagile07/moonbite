Explore traditional dishes from across Africa

North Africa

Couscous

It is a dish made from tiny steamed granules of semolina (wheat), usually served with vegetables, meat, or stew on top.

Shakshouka

It is a dish made of eggs gently poached in a spicy tomato sauce, often flavored with peppers, onions, garlic, and cumin. It is usually eaten with bread.

West Africa

Jollof Rice

It is a popular West African dish made by cooking rice in a rich tomato sauce with peppers, onions, and spices, often served with chicken, fish, or beef.

Egusi Soup

It is a rich, thick Nigerian soup made from grounded melon seeds. Its cooked with leafy vegetables, palm oil, spices, and often meat or fish, giving it a nutty flavor and hearty texture. Its commonly eaten with foods like pounded yam or fufu.

East Africa

Mandazi

It is a lightly sweet, deep-fried dough snack popular in East Africa. Its soft and fluffy inside, slightly crispy outside, and often flavored with coconut milk or cardamom.

Mshikaki

It is a popular food of marinated chunks of meat (usually beef) threaded onto skewers and grilled over charcoal. Its known for its spicy, smoky flavor and is often served with sauce, flatbread, or salad.

Southern Africa

Seswaa

It is a simple, traditional Botswana dish made by slow-boiling beef or goat with salt until very tender, then shredding it. Its usually served with pap (maize porridge) and sometimes greens.

Biltong

It is a cured, air-dried meat snack (usually beef) seasoned with vinegar, salt, and spices, then sliced into strips and eaten as a protein-rich snack.
